Flexbox Example Plants Animals Fungi Protista Monera Plants Bryophytes, commonly known as mosses Plants are mainly multicellular organisms, predominantly photosynthetic eukaryotes of the kingdom Plantae. Historically, plants were treated as one of two kingdoms including all living things that were not animals, and all algae and fungi were treated as plants. However, all current definitions of Plantae exclude the fungi and some algae, as well as the prokaryotes (the archaea and bacteria). By one definition, plants form the clade Viridiplantae (Latin name for "green plants"), a group that includes the flowering plants, conifers and other gymnosperms, ferns and their allies, hornworts, liverworts, mosses, and the green algae, but excludes the red and brown algae. Major plant groups Bryophytes (mosses) Pteridophytes (ferns) Gymnosperms (conifers) Angiosperms (flowering, seed-bearing plants) Note: Algae and fungi are not classified as plants.
